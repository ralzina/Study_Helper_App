What is Active Recall?
Active Recall is a learning method where you actively retrieve information from memory instead of passively reviewing notes. It strengthens memory retention and identifies knowledge gaps.
The Process
  • Study the Material: Set a timer to read or learn the topic thoroughly at your preferred time. When the timer starts running, the input boxes for minutes and seconds are all disabled. In the case that you have an unexpected reason to stop studying but don't want the timer to keep running, you can click pause to pause the timer and click start when you're ready to keep studying. In the case that you want to reset your timer because you have to leave or want to change the time, you can click reset and this button will reactivate the input boxes of the minutes and seconds.

  • Recall Without Notes: Test yourself by writing what you remember.

  • Review and Repeat: Export your writing by clicking the export button and compare your knowledge in the saved text file to the material, correct mistakes, and revisit challenging areas.
Benefits
  • Improves Retention: Retrieval practice strengthens memory.

  • Identifies Weaknesses: Reveals gaps in understanding.

  • Boosts Exam Readiness: Mimics real-world recall needs.
