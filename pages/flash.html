What are Flashcards?
Flashcards are a learning tool used to aid memory retention. They consist of a card with a question, word, or concept on one side and the corresponding answer or explanation on the other. Flashcards can be physical or digital, and they help learners reinforce knowledge.
The Process
  • Write a question or term on the front of the card.

  • Write the answer or explanation on the back.

  • Review the flashcards you created.

  • Test your knowledge with Test mode. In this mode, you can randomly go through your flashcards, and if you know the term or the answer you click Master. You can also retry each flashcard if you don't know it yet. On the top-right corner, you can see how many flashcards you have mastered. Once you master them all or click Stop, you will exit test mode.

  • Export your flashcards and save the file so that you can save your flashcards and have access to them by simply uploading the file in the quick access section.

Benefits
  • Promotes memory retention by forcing you to retrieve information from memory.

  • Provides a focused, interactive learning experience.

  • Easy to use anywhere, by exporting your file with the flashcards to save them.

  • Can be tailored to any subject or type of information.

  • Enhances learner engagement through self-testing, which can be more effective than passive study methods.
